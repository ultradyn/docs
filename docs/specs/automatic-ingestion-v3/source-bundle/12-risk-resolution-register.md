# Risk Resolution Register

This register records how the major issues are controlled. “Resolved” means converted into an enforceable invariant, fail-closed policy, reversible workflow, or measurable release gate—not that the underlying uncertainty has vanished.

| ID | Risk | Resolution | Residual condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| R1 | Universal semantic completeness is impossible | Bounded closure: forward obligations + Reverse Questioner + source ledger + human curiosity checkpoint + named snapshot/policy certificate. | A useful interpretation outside the declared boundary may still appear later and reopens the run. |
| R2 | External source bytes may disappear | Promotion requires a verified content-addressed replay capsule; external-only is exploratory. | Retention/deletion policy may intentionally make later replay unavailable. |
| R3 | Curiosity can explode combinatorially | Every automatic child owns a finite obligation; preflight estimates work; budget pauses honestly. | A finite corpus can still be expensive and may need partitioning. |
| R4 | Role-separated models share blind spots | Deterministic checks, alternate-model/human critical audits, reverse direction, and labeled evaluation. | Common-mode error cannot be eliminated; consequential disagreement fails closed. |
| R5 | Extraction hashes can preserve bad extraction | Capability tiers, original+normalized locators, independent audits, immutable repairs, and human verification for approximate formats. | Some visual meaning remains human-only or excluded. |
| R6 | Authority/deprecation may be unknowable from text | Project precedence policy, scoped contradiction search, historical preservation, named human owner. | Human authority can still be wrong but is explicit/reversible. |
| R7 | Dedup false merges hide knowledge | Non-destructive equivalence, unique contribution transfer, dual replay, and revocation/invalidation. | False convergence may delay work before detection. |
| R8 | Provider prompt caching is unstable | Correctness, recovery, and commercial baseline assume zero provider cache; cache is optional optimization. | Some corpora may remain too expensive and need narrower scope. |
| R9 | Grounded Q&A may be poor documentation | Claim layer + Information Architect + canonical-home plan + navigation/editorial review + partial-publish modes. | Editorial quality remains partly human judgment. |
| R10 | Privacy, secrets, licensing, and deletion | Preflight classification/rights, approved routing, access propagation, replay retention, dependency-aware erasure, publication scans. | Organizational/legal governance remains mandatory. |
| R11 | Live dashboard creates false transparency | Separate Activity/Evidence/Assurance; proof-linked status; no hidden-reasoning claims; comprehension tests. | Users may still over-trust automation. |
| R12 | Parent prose becomes stale | Revision-pinned claims/answers/documents, SCC readiness, dependency invalidation, provisional preview watermarking. | Late discoveries can still cause costly but targeted rewrites. |
| R13 | Question graph becomes accidental ontology | Distinct source, claim, question, answer, and document layers with explicit relationships. | Claim granularity requires iterative tuning. |
| R14 | Evidence criticism and curiosity compromise each other | Separate Evidence Critic and Curiosity Planner contexts/contracts; ablation tests. | More calls increase baseline latency/cost, measured against quality gain. |

## Release rule

A risk may be reopened by a failed acceptance test, observed incident, new source format, changed policy, model/provider drift, or a new constraint. Reopening creates a new decision version and never silently rewrites the historical record.
